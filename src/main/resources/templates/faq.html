Find answers to the most common questions about solar energy in India

Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity through the photovoltaic effect. When sunlight hits the silicon cells in solar panels, it knocks electrons loose, creating a flow of electricity. This DC electricity is then converted to AC by an inverter for home use.

India is ideally suited for solar power with over 300 sunny days annually in most regions. The country receives an average of 4-7 kWh per square meter per day of solar radiation, making it one of the best regions globally for solar power generation. Even during monsoon seasons, modern solar panels continue to generate electricity, though at somewhat reduced efficiency.

Most quality solar panels in India come with a 25-year performance warranty and typically have a lifespan of 25-30 years, even in India's varied climate conditions. High-quality panels are designed to withstand:

  • High temperatures during summer months
  • Heavy monsoon rainfall
  • Dust and pollution in urban areas

Premium panels typically degrade at a rate of only 0.5% to 0.7% per year in Indian conditions, meaning they'll still operate at 80-85% efficiency after 25 years. Inverters generally need replacement after 8-12 years, while the panels themselves are very durable with proper maintenance.

Regular cleaning is especially important in many parts of India due to dust accumulation, which can be more significant than in temperate regions.

Net metering allows you to export excess solar power to the grid and receive credits that offset your consumption when your solar system isn't producing enough. Here's how it works in India:

  • A special bi-directional meter is installed that tracks both import (from grid) and export (to grid) of electricity
  • You're billed only for the "net" electricity consumed (import minus export)
  • If you export more than you import in a billing cycle, most states allow the credits to carry forward to future bills

Net metering policies vary by state in India:

  • Some states offer 1:1 compensation (export credited at same rate as import)
  • Others use a reduced rate for exported electricity
  • Most states have capacity caps (usually up to 10kW for residential)
  • Settlement periods vary from monthly to annual depending on the state

We handle all paperwork and coordination with your local DISCOM for net meter installation and will explain the specific policy applicable in your area during consultation.

Whether you need batteries depends on your specific situation:

Grid-Tied Systems (Without Batteries):
  • Most common and affordable option in urban India
  • Works well in areas with reliable grid power and net metering
  • Does not provide backup during grid outages
  • Lower initial cost and maintenance
Hybrid Systems (With Batteries):
  • Provides power during grid outages
  • Essential in areas with frequent power cuts
  • Allows for energy storage and use during evening hours
  • Higher initial investment but greater energy independence

Battery technologies available in India include:

  • Lithium-ion: Higher cost but longer lifespan (10+ years) and better performance
  • Advanced Lead Acid: More affordable but shorter lifespan (3-5 years)
  • Tubular Batteries: Common in India, good balance of cost and durability

During your consultation, we'll analyze your power outage situation, energy usage patterns, and budget to recommend the most suitable solution for your needs.
