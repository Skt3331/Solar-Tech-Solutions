Solar Panel Maintenance Tips

Low Maintenance, High Performance

One of the greatest advantages of solar energy systems is their minimal maintenance requirements. With no moving parts and durable construction, solar panels are designed to operate reliably for decades with proper care. The following tips will help ensure your system continues to perform optimally throughout its lifespan.

Regular Inspection & Monitoring

Monitor Your System's Performance

Regularly check your system's monitoring app or portal to track energy production. Look for:

  • Consistent daily production patterns (accounting for seasonal differences)
  • Any unexpected drops in generation
  • Error messages or alerts from the inverter
Frequency: Weekly check recommended

Visual Inspection

Periodically do a visual check of your system from ground level. Look for:

  • Visible physical damage to panels
  • Debris accumulation on panels
  • Loose mounting hardware
  • Signs of water infiltration around roof penetrations
Frequency: Monthly and after severe weather events

Safety First

Never attempt to climb onto your roof to inspect or clean panels unless you have proper safety equipment and experience. For most maintenance tasks, it's safer to contact a professional.

Cleaning Your Solar Panels

While rain naturally cleans panels in most climates, occasional manual cleaning may be necessary to maintain optimal performance, especially in dry, dusty regions or areas with high pollen.

When to Clean
  • When you notice visible dirt, dust, bird droppings, or pollen buildup
  • When system monitoring shows unexplained decreased performance
  • During extended periods without rain
  • After nearby construction work that created dust
DIY Cleaning Methods

If your panels are accessible from the ground or a safe position:

Recommended Supplies:
  • Soft brush or soft cloth
  • Squeegee with extended handle
  • Mild soap (if needed)
  • Hose with spray nozzle
  • Deionized water (to prevent mineral deposits)
Cleaning Steps:
  1. Clean panels early morning or evening when they're cool
  2. Rinse panels with water to remove loose debris
  3. If necessary, use a mild soap solution and soft brush
  4. Thoroughly rinse off all soap
  5. Use a squeegee to prevent water spots (optional)
What to Avoid
  • High-pressure washers

    Can damage seals and void warranties

  • Abrasive materials or tools

    Can scratch panel surfaces

  • Harsh chemicals or detergents

    Can damage anti-reflective coatings

  • Hot water on hot panels

    Thermal shock can crack glass

  • Walking on panels

    Can cause microfractures and damage

Seasonal Maintenance Considerations

Winter

  • Monitor for snow accumulation that blocks panels
  • Let snow melt naturally - panels' dark surface accelerates melting
  • Only remove snow with a soft snow rake designed for solar panels
  • Check system performance after heavy snowfall
  • Ensure inverter area remains clear of snow and accessible

Fall

  • Clear fallen leaves that may accumulate on panels
  • Trim overhanging branches that could drop debris
  • Check mounting hardware before winter weather arrives
  • Schedule professional inspection if needed
  • Monitor for accumulation of seasonal pollens or seeds

Summer

  • Check for dust buildup during dry periods
  • Ensure proper ventilation around inverters to prevent overheating
  • Monitor performance during peak production season
  • Check for any new shading issues from growing vegetation
  • Consider cleaning before the highest production months

Spring

  • Check for winter damage after snow and ice season
  • Clean pollen that may accumulate on panels
  • Inspect roof penetrations and flashing after freeze/thaw cycles
  • Trim new vegetation growth that may cause shading
  • Verify monitoring system is functioning properly

When to Call a Professional

While solar systems are generally low-maintenance, certain situations require professional attention:

System Performance Issues
  • Significant unexpected drop in energy production
  • Error messages from inverter or monitoring system
  • System shutdowns or failure to restart
Electrical Issues
  • Exposed or damaged wiring
  • Signs of overheating at connection points
  • Circuit breakers frequently tripping
Physical Damage
  • Cracked or broken panels
  • Loose or damaged mounting hardware
  • Water leakage around roof penetrations
Routine Maintenance
  • Annual professional inspections (recommended)
  • Inverter service (per manufacturer guidelines)
  • Professional cleaning for inaccessible panels
