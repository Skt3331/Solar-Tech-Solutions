Energy Storage Solutions

Maximizing Your Solar Investment with Energy Storage

Energy storage systems allow you to store excess solar energy for use when the sun isn't shining, providing greater energy independence and maximizing your return on investment.

Benefits of Energy Storage

Energy Independence

Reduce reliance on the utility grid and protect against power outages with reliable backup power.

Cost Savings

Avoid expensive peak electricity rates by using stored energy during high-demand periods.

Maximize Solar Usage

Store excess daytime production for use at night instead of sending it back to the grid.

Environmental Impact

Reduce your carbon footprint by using more of your clean solar energy and less from the grid.

Types of Energy Storage Solutions

Lithium-Ion Batteries
Features:
  • High energy density - more power in less space
  • Long cycle life (5,000-10,000 cycles)
  • Low maintenance requirements
  • High charge/discharge efficiency (90-95%)
  • Rapid response time for power demands

Ideal For: Residential installations, daily cycling, and smart energy management.

Popular Brands: Tesla Powerwall, LG Chem RESU, Enphase Encharge

Lead-Acid Batteries
Features:
  • Lower upfront cost than lithium-ion
  • Established technology with proven reliability
  • Shorter cycle life (500-1,200 cycles)
  • Requires regular maintenance
  • Lower energy density - takes up more space

Ideal For: Budget-conscious users, backup power applications with infrequent cycling.

Types: Flooded Lead-Acid (FLA), Absorbed Glass Mat (AGM), Gel

Saltwater Batteries
Features:
  • Non-toxic and environmentally friendly
  • No risk of fire or explosion
  • 100% recyclable materials
  • Long lifespan with minimal degradation
  • Lower energy density than lithium-ion

Ideal For: Environmentally conscious users, applications where safety is a primary concern.

Example: Aquion Energy Aspen batteries

Sizing Your Energy Storage System

The right battery size depends on your energy needs and goals:

  • Backup Power Only: If you simply want protection during outages, a smaller system focused on powering essential loads may be sufficient.
  • Self-Consumption: To maximize use of your solar energy, size your storage based on your average evening and nighttime usage.
  • Going Off-Grid: For total independence, you'll need enough storage to cover multiple days of usage during periods of limited sun.
