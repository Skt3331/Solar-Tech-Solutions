Solar Tax Incentives & Rebates

Important Notice

Tax incentives and rebate programs change regularly. This information is current as of 2023, but we recommend consulting with a tax professional for the most up-to-date information regarding your specific situation.

Federal Solar Investment Tax Credit (ITC)

The Federal Solar Investment Tax Credit (ITC) is one of the most significant incentives for solar energy in the United States.

Current ITC Rates:
  • 30% tax credit for systems installed in 2022-2032
  • 26% tax credit for systems installed in 2033
  • 22% tax credit for systems installed in 2034
  • The tax credit expires after 2034 unless renewed by Congress
What's Covered:
  • Solar photovoltaic (PV) panels
  • Labor costs for installation
  • System equipment (inverters, mounting hardware, wiring)
  • Energy storage devices (batteries) charged by the solar PV panels
  • Sales taxes on eligible expenses
Example Savings

For a $20,000 solar system:

System Cost $20,000
30% Federal Tax Credit -$6,000
Net Cost $14,000
How to Claim the Federal Tax Credit
  1. Install a qualifying solar energy system
  2. Complete and attach IRS Form 5695 to your federal tax return
  3. The credit is applied to your income tax liability for the year of installation
  4. If the tax credit exceeds your tax liability, the excess can be carried forward to future tax years

State & Local Incentives

Many states, utilities, and local governments offer additional incentives that can dramatically reduce the cost of going solar.

State Tax Credits

Many states offer their own tax credits that can be claimed in addition to the federal ITC. These typically range from 10-25% of system costs with varying caps by state.

Examples: New York, Arizona, Massachusetts, and Iowa offer state solar tax credits.

Cash Rebates

Some states and utilities offer upfront rebates that can reduce system costs immediately. These are typically available for a limited time or until a specific program cap is reached.

Examples: California, Colorado, and New York have significant rebate programs.

Property Tax Exemptions

Many states exempt the added home value from solar installations from property tax assessments, meaning your property taxes won't increase despite the increase in home value.

Examples: Florida, Texas, and Arizona offer 100% exemptions.

Sales Tax Exemptions

Several states exempt solar energy equipment from state sales tax, saving you 4-8% on your system cost depending on local tax rates.

Examples: Washington, New Jersey, and Massachusetts have sales tax exemptions.

Financing Programs

Various financing options can help make solar more accessible with low or no upfront costs.

PACE Financing

Property Assessed Clean Energy (PACE) programs allow you to finance solar through an assessment on your property tax bill, often requiring no money down and providing longer payment terms (up to 20 years).

Solar Loans

Many states and utilities offer low-interest or zero-down solar loan programs specifically designed for renewable energy projects, often with better terms than traditional loans.
